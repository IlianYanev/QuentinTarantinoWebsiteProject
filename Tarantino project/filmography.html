1. Reservoir Dogs 1992

Six criminals, hired to steal diamonds, do not know each other's true identity. When the heist is ambushed by police, they suspect that one of them is an informant. Tension rises as they begin to distrust one another and unravel the mystery of who betrayed them, all while trying to escape with the loot. This tense and gritty crime drama keeps you on the edge of your seat as it explores themes of loyalty, betrayal, and survival.

2. Pulp Fiction 1994

The lives of two mob hitmen, Vincent Vega and Jules Winnfield, intertwine with the fates of a boxer, a gangster's wife, and a pair of diner bandits. In Quentin Tarantino’s iconic film, the stories unfold in a nonlinear fashion, exposing the chaotic and violent world of crime, loyalty, betrayal, and redemption. From Vincent and Jules' philosophical conversations about life and death to Butch the boxer’s attempt to escape his violent past, the characters’ paths cross in unexpected and darkly comic ways.

At the heart of Pulp Fiction is the relationship between Vincent and Jules, who find themselves at a crossroads in their hitman careers after a botched job. Their philosophical debates about good and evil serve as a backdrop to their violent and unpredictable lives. Meanwhile, Butch, a boxer who is paid to take a dive in a fixed match, decides to double-cross his employer, leading to a series of bloody and fateful encounters.

8.9/10

3. Jackie Brown 1997

Jackie Brown, a struggling flight attendant, finds herself trapped between the law and a ruthless arms dealer she’s been working for on the side. When she’s caught smuggling money into the U.S., she’s forced to cooperate with federal agents to bring down her employer, Ordell Robbie. However, Jackie, resourceful and bold, has plans of her own. She devises a dangerous scheme to outwit both the police and Ordell, hoping to escape her precarious situation with a hefty payday.

As the story unfolds, Jackie teams up with Max Cherry, a sympathetic bail bondsman who becomes captivated by her charm and cunning. Together, they navigate a web of deceit, betrayal, and unexpected alliances. Jackie’s plan involves a complex money exchange, playing the FBI, Ordell, and his associates against one another in a high-stakes game of survival. The film explores themes of loyalty, greed, and empowerment, showcasing Jackie’s resilience as she takes control of her destiny.

7.5/10

4. Kill Bill 2003

A tale of revenge, resilience, and redemption, *Kill Bill* follows The Bride, a former assassin who awakens from a four-year coma after being betrayed and left for dead by her employer and lover, Bill. Fueled by vengeance, she embarks on a blood-soaked journey to eliminate the members of her former assassination squad, the Deadly Viper Assassination Squad, and ultimately confront Bill himself.

Directed by Quentin Tarantino and starring Uma Thurman in one of her most iconic roles, *Kill Bill* is a two-part masterpiece celebrated for its unique style, unforgettable characters, and relentless energy.

8.1/10 (Vol. 1) & 8.0/10 (Vol. 2)

5. Death Proof 2007

In *Death Proof*, Quentin Tarantino delivers a thrilling homage to the grindhouse films of the 1970s. The film follows Stuntman Mike, a psychopathic stunt driver who uses his "death-proof" car to carry out his twisted fantasies. Divided into two distinct acts, the movie explores Mike’s interactions with two groups of unsuspecting women, leading to a chilling and explosive finale.

The first act introduces a group of young women enjoying a night out in Austin, Texas, unaware that they are being stalked by the sinister Stuntman Mike. Using his modified car, Mike orchestrates a horrifying crash that leaves him unscathed but results in deadly consequences for the women. The second act shifts to another group of women, including a stuntwoman named Zoë Bell playing herself. What starts as a fun test drive of a vintage car turns into a life-or-death chase when Mike targets them. However, this time, his victims fight back with ferocity, turning the hunter into the hunted.

7.0/10

6. Inglourious Basterds 2009

*Inglourious Basterds* is a bold reimagining of history, set during World War II, where Quentin Tarantino masterfully blends tension, humor, and violence. The film follows two converging storylines: one centered on a ruthless group of Jewish-American soldiers known as "The Basterds," and the other on a young Jewish woman, Shosanna Dreyfus, who narrowly escaped a massacre orchestrated by the sadistic SS officer Colonel Hans Landa.

The Basterds, led by the charismatic and unrelenting Lieutenant Aldo Raine, embark on a brutal mission to strike fear into the hearts of the Nazis by assassinating high-ranking officers. Meanwhile, Shosanna, now living under a false identity in Nazi-occupied France, runs a small cinema. When an opportunity arises to exact revenge on the Nazi regime, she crafts an intricate plan involving the premiere of a propaganda film attended by top Nazi officials, including Adolf Hitler.

8.3/10

Upcomming projects

Quentin Tarantino’s canceled project ''The Movie Critic'' was intended to be his tenth and final film, set in 1977 California. The story would follow a real but obscure film critic who reviewed mainstream films in adult magazines, drawing influence from the gritty style of characters like Travis Bickle from Taxi Driver. Tarantino had initially cast Brad Pitt, potentially reprising his Once Upon a Time in Hollywood role, but ultimately decided to scrap the film, leaving fans speculating on what his true final movie will be​.
